# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating PMs for senior roles. These visitors come with a specific question: "Can this person ship real products and lead teams?" They spend seconds making first judgments and want proof, not narrative flourish.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that demonstrates real scope—0-to-1 launches, platform growth, and cross-functional leadership—in a way that shows, not tells. The work speaks for itself: which problems were solved, how, with what impact, and what that says about how you think. Success is a recruiter or founder deciding to reach out.

## Positioning

I ship real products at meaningful scale.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA**: Contact (reach out via email or LinkedIn to explore opportunities)
- **Secondary CTA**: Explore specific case studies to understand how problems were approached
- **The line a visitor remembers**: "Shipped products across mobility, creator economy, and AI—from 0-to-1 launches to scaling platforms used by thousands."
- **Belief ladder**: 
  1. This person has shipped real products that grew/mattered (case study outcomes, scale metrics)
  2. They solve problems others abandon (approach, decision-making, messy reality narratives)
  3. People want to work with them again (testimonials, cross-functional collaboration patterns)
- **Proof on hand**: Case studies with outcomes (mobility, creator economy, AI domains); testimonials from collaborators; resume with scope and impact

## Brand Personality

**Confident, thoughtful, and playfully creative.** No hedging or false modesty, but every detail earned its place. The portfolio feels like a person who knows their own judgment, respects the reader's time, and has a point of view. Clear about what was shipped and why it mattered. The design has a creative spark—carefully chosen typographic moves, interactive moments that delight, unexpected details—but never at the expense of clarity. Playful, not flashy. Tasteful, not trendy.

## Anti-references

- **Overly polished/buttoned-up corporate feel**: Not a stock resume in website form; avoid slick corporate speak or hollow credentials
- **Trendy or flashy design-focused**: The design should disappear; the work should not compete with visual effects
- **Vague or process-focused storytelling**: No case studies about the journey of discovery—show the actual problem, decision, and impact
- **Generic PM advice or metrics-obsessed**: This is a portfolio of work, not a Medium article or talk about PM frameworks

## Design Principles

1. **Let the work speak**: Design and copy are transparent; they don't draw attention to themselves. The case studies and outcomes are the star.
2. **Respect visitor time**: Quick to scan, fast to load, immediately clear. No filler, no fluff, no "scroll down for more."
3. **Craft over flash**: Typography, color, and rhythm are meticulous. Motion is intentional. Elegance through restraint.
4. **Show, don't tell**: Real problems, real outcomes, real testimonials—not adjectives or promises.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline with good defaults. Keyboard navigation, semantic markup, color contrast, and readable typography. Reduced-motion support already in place per the theme engine.
